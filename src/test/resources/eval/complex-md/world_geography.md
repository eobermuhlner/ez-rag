## Major Biomes

Earth's land surface is covered by several distinct biomes, each characterised by its
climate, vegetation, and wildlife:

- **Tropical Rainforest**
  - Located near the equator (Amazon, Congo, Southeast Asia)
  - High rainfall (>2,000 mm/year) and stable warm temperatures
  - Highest biodiversity of any terrestrial biome
  - Threatened by deforestation at approximately 4.7 million hectares per year
- **Savanna**
  - Tropical grasslands with scattered trees
  - Distinct wet and dry seasons
  - Found in sub-Saharan Africa, parts of Brazil (Cerrado), and northern Australia
- **Desert**
  - Receive less than 250 mm of precipitation annually
  - Cover about 33% of Earth's land surface
  - Types: hot (Sahara, Arabian), cold (Gobi, Antarctic)
- **Temperate Forest**
  - Deciduous and mixed forests of the mid-latitudes
  - Distinct four seasons; annual precipitation 750–1,500 mm
  - Covers much of Europe, eastern North America, and eastern Asia
- **Boreal Forest (Taiga)**
  - Largest terrestrial biome by area
  - Coniferous trees (spruce, fir, pine) dominate
  - Extends across Canada, Russia, and Scandinavia
- **Tundra**
  - Arctic and alpine regions above the treeline
  - Permafrost layer beneath the surface
  - Very short growing season (6–10 weeks)

## Mountain Ranges

The world's major mountain ranges and their highest peaks:

- **Himalayas** (Asia)
  - Contains all 14 peaks exceeding 8,000 m
  - Mount Everest: 8,849 m — highest point on Earth
  - Formed by collision of the Indian and Eurasian tectonic plates
- **Andes** (South America)
  - Longest continental mountain range: ~7,000 km
  - Aconcagua: 6,961 m — highest peak outside Asia
  - Active volcanism along much of its length
- **Rocky Mountains** (North America)
  - Extend ~4,800 km from British Columbia to New Mexico
  - Mount Elbert: 4,401 m — highest peak
  - Continental Divide runs along the crest
- **Alps** (Europe)
  - Extend ~1,200 km across eight countries
  - Mont Blanc: 4,808 m — highest peak in Western Europe
  - Origin of many major European rivers (Rhine, Rhône, Po)
