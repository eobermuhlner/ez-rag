# Capital Cities of the World

This document describes the capital cities of countries around the world, including their geography, history, population, and notable landmarks.

## Paris

Paris is the capital of France and one of Europe's most populous cities, with a metropolitan area of over 12 million people. It is located on the Seine River in north-central France. The city is famous for iconic landmarks including the Eiffel Tower, the Louvre Museum which houses the Mona Lisa, Notre-Dame Cathedral, and the Arc de Triomphe. Paris has been a center of art, culture, fashion, and gastronomy for centuries and hosts tens of millions of tourists every year. The city's subway system, the Métro, opened in 1900 and now covers 16 lines and over 300 stations.

## Berlin

Berlin is the capital of Germany and its largest city, with approximately 3.7 million residents. It is a city-state that is simultaneously a state of the Federal Republic. Berlin became the reunified capital after the fall of the Berlin Wall in November 1989 and formal German reunification in October 1990, when the government relocated from Bonn. The city is known for its turbulent 20th-century history, vibrant arts and nightclub scene, and over 170 museums. The Brandenburg Gate, the Berlin Wall Memorial and East Side Gallery, and Museum Island are among its most visited sites. Berlin is also a major center of technology startups in Europe.

## Tokyo

Tokyo is the capital of Japan and the seat of the Japanese imperial family and government. The Greater Tokyo Area, which includes Tokyo and several surrounding prefectures, is the most populous metropolitan area in the world with over 37 million people. Tokyo is a global center of technology, fashion, finance, and food culture; the city has more Michelin-starred restaurants than any other city in the world. It hosted the Summer Olympics in 1964 and again in 2021, and the Paralympics in both years. The Shinjuku ward in Tokyo has one of the world's busiest railway stations, handling over 3.5 million passengers per day.

## Canberra

Canberra is the capital of Australia, chosen as a compromise between rival cities Sydney and Melbourne. It was purpose-built as the national capital following federation in 1901, with construction beginning in 1913 based on a design by American architects Walter Burley Griffin and Marion Mahony Griffin. The name Canberra is thought to derive from an Aboriginal word meaning meeting place. The city is home to Australia's Parliament House, the Australian War Memorial, the National Gallery of Australia, and the National Museum of Australia. Its population is approximately 470,000, making it Australia's largest inland city.

## Brasilia

Brasilia is the capital of Brazil and was constructed from scratch in just 41 months before replacing Rio de Janeiro as the capital in April 1960. The project was initiated by President Juscelino Kubitschek as a statement of national ambition and modernization. The city was designed by urban planner Lúcio Costa and architect Oscar Niemeyer, who created its distinctive brutalist and modernist architecture, including the National Congress building with its twin towers and distinctive dome and bowl. Brasilia is a UNESCO World Heritage Site, recognized for its contribution to modern urban planning and architecture. It is located in the Federal District in Brazil's central highlands at an altitude of about 1,172 meters.

## Ottawa

Ottawa is the capital of Canada and is located in the province of Ontario on the south bank of the Ottawa River, directly across from Gatineau, Quebec. It was chosen as the capital in 1857 by Queen Victoria as a compromise between the rival cities of Toronto and Montreal, and partly for its strategic inland location away from the American border. Ottawa is home to Parliament Hill with the Peace Tower, Rideau Hall (the residence of the Governor General), the Supreme Court of Canada, and the Rideau Canal, which is a UNESCO World Heritage Site and the world's largest naturally refrigerated skating rink in winter. The population of the Ottawa-Gatineau metropolitan area is approximately 1.4 million.

## Madrid

Madrid is the capital of Spain and the country's largest city, with a population of over 3.3 million in the city proper and approximately 6.7 million in the metropolitan area. Located at an elevation of 667 meters above sea level, it is the highest capital city in the European Union and has one of the highest elevations of any European capital. Madrid sits on the Manzanares River in the geographic center of the Iberian Peninsula. Its major attractions include the Prado Museum, which holds one of the finest collections of European art in the world, the Reina Sofía Museum with Picasso's Guernica, the Royal Palace, and the lively Retiro Park. Real Madrid, one of the world's most successful football clubs, is based in the city.

## Rome

Rome is the capital of Italy and has been a continuously inhabited city for over 2,700 years, earning it the nickname the Eternal City. It served as the center of the Roman Empire at its height and later became the seat of the Roman Catholic Church and the Papacy. The city contains the sovereign state of Vatican City within its boundaries. Rome's historic treasures include the Colosseum, the Roman Forum, the Pantheon, the Trevi Fountain, and the Spanish Steps. The city's historic center is a UNESCO World Heritage Site. Rome hosted the Summer Olympics in 1960 and has a current population of approximately 2.8 million.

## London

London is the capital of the United Kingdom and England, situated on the River Thames in southeastern England. Founded by the Romans as Londinium in approximately AD 43, it has grown to be one of the world's most important cities with a population of approximately 9 million within the city proper. Key landmarks include the Tower of London (a UNESCO World Heritage Site), Buckingham Palace, the Houses of Parliament with Elizabeth Tower housing the bell known as Big Ben, the British Museum, and the Tate Modern gallery. London is a global financial center, home to the London Stock Exchange, Lloyd's of London, and the headquarters of numerous multinational banks and insurance companies. The London Underground, opened in 1863, was the world's first underground railway.

## Washington D.C.

Washington, D.C. is the capital of the United States of America. It is a federal district not belonging to any state, established in 1790 as a compromise location on the Potomac River between the northern and southern states. The city is home to all three branches of the federal government: the White House (executive), the United States Capitol (legislative), and the Supreme Court (judicial). The National Mall, a two-mile open parkway, runs from the Lincoln Memorial to the Capitol and is flanked by Smithsonian museums and monuments including the Washington Monument, the Vietnam Veterans Memorial, and the National Museum of American History. Washington, D.C. has a resident population of approximately 700,000, while the broader metropolitan area has over 6 million residents.

## Beijing

Beijing is the capital of the People's Republic of China and one of the world's most populous cities, with over 21 million residents in the municipality. The city has served as China's capital for much of the past eight centuries, first under the Mongol Yuan dynasty and then through the Ming and Qing dynasties. The Forbidden City, a vast palace complex that served as the home of Chinese emperors for nearly 500 years, lies at the very heart of Beijing and is now a UNESCO World Heritage Site containing almost 1,000 buildings. The Great Wall of China, another UNESCO site, passes through the mountains north of the city. Beijing hosted the Summer Olympics in 2008 and the Winter Olympics in 2022, becoming the first city to have hosted both.

## Moscow

Moscow is the capital of Russia and the largest city in Europe, with a population of approximately 12.5 million within the city and over 17 million in the greater metropolitan area. It has served as the political, economic, and cultural center of Russia and previously of the Soviet Union for centuries. The Moscow Kremlin, a fortified complex on the banks of the Moskva River, serves as the official residence of the Russian president and contains several cathedrals, palaces, and museums. Red Square, adjacent to the Kremlin, contains the colorful Saint Basil's Cathedral, the State Historical Museum, and Lenin's Mausoleum. The Moscow Metro system is famous for its ornate stations featuring chandeliers, mosaics, and sculptures, and carries around 6.5 million passengers daily.

## Cairo

Cairo is the capital of Egypt and the largest city in Africa and the Arab world, with a greater metropolitan population of over 20 million people. The city straddles the Nile River in northeastern Africa and has been inhabited for millennia. The ancient city of Memphis and the Giza Plateau with the Great Pyramid of Giza, the Great Sphinx, and the Pyramid of Khafre are located on the outskirts of modern Cairo. The Great Pyramid, built around 2560 BC for Pharaoh Khufu, stood as the tallest man-made structure in the world for over 3,800 years. The Egyptian Museum in central Cairo houses one of the world's largest and most significant collections of ancient Egyptian artifacts, including the treasures of Tutankhamun.

## New Delhi

New Delhi is the capital of India and is situated within the larger National Capital Territory of Delhi, which has a population of over 30 million, making it one of the world's most populous urban areas. New Delhi was designed by British architects Edwin Lutyens and Herbert Baker and officially became the capital of British India in 1931, replacing Calcutta. Prominent landmarks include India Gate, a war memorial for soldiers who died in World War I, the President's Estate (Rashtrapati Bhavan), Parliament House, and the nearby Qutb Minar, a medieval minaret and UNESCO World Heritage Site. Old Delhi, the walled city built by Mughal Emperor Shah Jahan in the 17th century, contains the Red Fort and Jama Masjid Mosque. India Gate sits at the center of a ceremonial boulevard and is visited by millions each year.

## Mexico City

Mexico City is the capital of Mexico and one of the most populous cities in the Western Hemisphere, with a metropolitan population of over 21 million. The site was originally the Aztec city of Tenochtitlan, founded in 1325 on an island in Lake Texcoco. Spanish conquistadors under Hernán Cortés conquered the city in 1521 and constructed a new city on its ruins. The historic center of Mexico City contains the Zócalo (officially the Plaza de la Constitución), one of the largest city squares in the world, the Metropolitan Cathedral begun in 1573, and the National Palace. Mexico City sits at an altitude of approximately 2,250 meters above sea level in a highland basin surrounded by mountains and volcanoes. Nearby Teotihuacan, with its Pyramid of the Sun and Pyramid of the Moon, was one of the largest cities in the pre-Columbian Americas.

## Seoul

Seoul is the capital of South Korea and has a metropolitan population of approximately 25 million, making it one of the largest and most densely populated urban agglomerations in the world. It has been the capital of Korea for over 600 years, since the Joseon dynasty designated it as the capital in 1394 under the name Hanyang. Modern Seoul is a global leader in technology, entertainment (K-pop and Korean cinema), and design. Gyeongbokgung Palace, the main royal palace of the Joseon dynasty built in 1395, is one of the most visited tourist sites in the country. The Han River, which flows through the heart of Seoul, is flanked by parks, cycling paths, and recreational areas. Seoul hosted the Summer Olympics in 1988 and the FIFA World Cup in 2002.

## Nairobi

Nairobi is the capital of Kenya and the largest city in East Africa, with a population of approximately 4.4 million in the city and over 9 million in the metropolitan area. It was founded in 1899 as a supply depot for the Uganda Railway and became the capital of the British East Africa Protectorate in 1905, and subsequently of independent Kenya in 1963. Nairobi is one of the few capital cities in the world with a national park within its boundaries: Nairobi National Park is home to lions, leopards, cheetahs, rhinoceroses, giraffes, and hundreds of bird species within sight of the city skyline. The city is a major hub for international organizations operating in Africa, including UNEP (United Nations Environment Programme) and UN-Habitat, which maintain their headquarters there.

## Stockholm

Stockholm is the capital of Sweden and is built on 14 islands at the point where Lake Mälaren empties into the Baltic Sea. With a city population of about 975,000 and a metropolitan area of 2.4 million, it is the most populous city in Scandinavia. The old town, Gamla Stan, dates from the 13th century and contains the Royal Palace, Stockholm Cathedral, and narrow cobblestone streets lined with medieval buildings. The Nobel Prize Award Ceremony and Banquet take place annually at the Stockholm Concert Hall and Stockholm City Hall respectively, honoring laureates in physics, chemistry, medicine, literature, and economics. Stockholm is also home to globally recognized brands including ABBA, Spotify, H&M, IKEA (founded nearby), and Ericsson.

## Vienna

Vienna is the capital of Austria and was historically the imperial capital of the Habsburg Empire, one of the dominant European powers for over five centuries. Situated on the Danube River, Vienna has a current population of approximately 1.9 million. It is internationally known as the City of Music because of its extraordinary musical heritage: Ludwig van Beethoven, Johannes Brahms, Joseph Haydn, Gustav Mahler, Wolfgang Amadeus Mozart, Franz Schubert, and Johann Strauss II all lived and worked in Vienna. The historic center, including the Ringstrasse boulevard with its grand 19th-century public buildings and the Baroque Schönbrunn Palace, is a UNESCO World Heritage Site. Vienna consistently ranks as one of the world's most livable cities in quality-of-life surveys.

## Athens

Athens is the capital of Greece and one of the world's oldest continuously inhabited cities, with a recorded history of over 3,400 years. With a population of approximately 665,000 in the city proper and 3.7 million in the metropolitan area, it is the largest city in Greece. The Acropolis, a rocky hill above the city, is crowned by the Parthenon, a temple to the goddess Athena built between 447 and 438 BC; it remains one of the world's most enduring symbols of ancient civilization. Athens was the birthplace of democracy (under Cleisthenes in 508 BC), the philosophical tradition of Socrates, Plato, and Aristotle, Western drama, and many foundational principles of mathematics and science. The city hosted the first modern Olympic Games in 1896 and again in 2004.
